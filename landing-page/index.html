The purpose of Immigrant News is to offer a platform for immigrants to inform and be informed about safety issues that undocumented immigrants may face.

Conceptualization

The internet and social networks such as Facebook allow news affecting a community to spread fast, through established news sources such as the New York Times, new digital news outlets such as Salon, by citizen-journalists unaffiliated with public or private institutions, and through personal testimony published in personal social media accounts. In recent years checking the accuracy of news before false claims are spread has become very difficult due to the speed at which news can be shared, and the number of people it can be shared with. Both documented and undocumented immigrants can be susceptible to false claims due to the lack of official news sources regarding federal enforcement of immigration, and due to the way information is distributed unevenly throughout the community.

Proposal

Immigrant News aims to offer a platform where immigrants can find, share and verify truthful information about immigration activity and related news around the country. It aims to tackle the specific problem of verifying news and stopping rumors for a community that is always seeking reliable and up to date information in a time of political uncertainty. The application will allow users to log in and start interacting with the application by either verifying news or contributing with their own news. Immigrant News will have a reliability level assigned to each user. When someone is registered for the first time, reliability is set to ‘low’ and as the user interacts with the app, by verifying news correctly and marking incorrect news as false, reliability level will change accordingly. If a news item has been marked as true by a certain amount of people, the news can be considered truthful.

Personas

We created personas to imagine the users who would be using our application. We focused on two personas: Marta, an undocumented immigrant woman in her 40’s who sees immigration enforcement activities at work, and Shreyas, a documented immigrant who witnesses a detention at work.

Marta

Marta reads rumors on Facebook about possible detentions, and sees pictures that depict arrests, but is unable to corroborate the rumors, and still gets anxious about the news. She hears about stories of arrests and detentions directly from her co-workers, so she is able to corroborate those stories. She logs in regularly into Immigrant News to see if other people have written about the rumors she has heard, and to contribute what she has heard.

She reads on Facebook that immigration authorities are actively checking US visas and immigration documents of random travelers in the Los Angeles International Airport, and she is worried because she is traveling there for a family wedding in a month. She logs into Immigrant News to see if other people have reported this rumor and if anyone has approved the rumor. She searches for incidents at LAX, and finds out that the rumor of ICE being active in the airport has been debunked, and feels more confident about her travels.

Shreyas

Shreyas is not undocumented, but has family members or friends who are undocumented. He is in his mid-20’s, and works at a factory that creates electronic parts. One of his friends, Arjun, was detained after an immigration raid at his workplace, a factory in the suburbs of Chicago. ICE (Immigration, Customs, and Enforcement) agents went to the factory looking for one employee who did not show up to a court hearing, and in the process of looking for the employee, asked other employees at the factory their immigration status. Arjun was honest and told ICE that he was undocumented, and they arrested him for further questioning.

Shreyas is concerned that Arjun will be deported, and that Arjun will have to leave his wife and 3 year old child. He also wants other people to know that ICE is conducting raids that include non-targeted immigrants. He logs onto Immigrant News to report the incident, and sees that other users have added information about similar raids in the Chicago area. It appears to be a trend. He contacts the local news about this issue, and local reporters begin questioning factories and the ICE agency in the area.

Gathering Requirements

In addition to creating user personas, we conducted a focus group interview with three individuals, self-identified as immigrants, for the requirements gathering phase. Our interview included both structured and unstructured questions with the objective to get answers for specific doubts as well as to collect unsolicited and useful clues and advice. During the focus group, we split the process into three parts by discussing the project abstract, the project proposal, and finally the personas that we developed for the project. After discussing each part, we asked prepared questions, and asked impromptu questions.

After the requirements gathering phase, we decided to incorporate the following 6 requirements into our design challenges and final prototype.

Location

The web application will allow the user to search for news by keyword, date, and/or location

Details

The web application will display details of a story when the user selects a story from the news feed

Community Contributions

The web application will display an anonymous list of users who have contributed to a story and their respective trust rating

Add Stories

The web application will allow the user to contribute a new story to the database.

Validation

The web application will allow users to validate or invalidate a story.

Personal Additions

The web application will allow users to contribute to an existing story (contribute to the story with details or media (i.e. pictures), add comments, react, etc.)

Design + Sketches

We scheduled in-person meetings to create our sketches. All team members were in attendance and active participants during the sketch process. We went through a sketching and design process for 4 design challenges that we chose from our requirements. We will highlight the design process for one of our challenges, and the details of the rest are available here.

Design Challenge: Allow the user to search for news by keywords, date and/or location or in between two points.

The Process: For each iteration of our design challenge, we allotted ourselves 5 minutes to sketch as many ideas we had for the given challenge. After the 5 minutes, we presented our sketch ideas. Thereafter, we critiqued each other’s sketches and recorded the critiques which would help focus the design sketches for the next iteration. We followed this process for each iteration until we converged to one design sketch. The final sketch was typically drawn by one team member.

Formative Evaluation

We created a low-fidelity prototype of our application in order to conduct an evaluation before development. We conducted our formative evaluation with three students from UIC. Each of them were given a task card at a time for each of the three tasks. We will go into the evaluation process for one of the tasks, and a description of the rest of the process is available here.


Selected Formative Evaluation Results

Task 1: On the Immigrant News site, search for immigration enforcement related incidents at LAX airport. Find whether there is a related story in the search results newsfeed.

Two out of three users were not able to find the advanced search, so it needs to be more visible. One potential way to make it more visible is to add an “Advanced Search” blue hyperlink as most users are typically familiar with.

One user expressed confusion about the percentages next to each story that represent how trustworthy each story is. One possible improvement is to change the percentage just to the a number of people who have validated and invalidated it. The assumption here is that with this improvement, the color coding of the stories will become clear.
